Soy Sauce Ramen

Quick and easy ramen

Serving Size: 4

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 30 minutes

Scallion-Garlic Oil Ingredients:

  • 2 scallions, thinly sliced
  • 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup neutral oil, such as canola or grapeseed

Broth Ingredients:

  • 4 cups clam juice
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt

For Serving Ingredients:

  • 4 teaspoons toasted sesame oil
  • 20 ounces fresh ramen noodles
  • 4 soft-boiled eggs
  • 16 slices Cha Shu (braised pork belly, optional)
  • 1 scallion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into 4 equal slices

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
  2. MAKE THE SCALLION_GARLIC OIL: In a small skillet, combine the scallions, garlic, and neutral oil. Cook over low heat until the harlic turns golden, about 15 minutes. Set aside.
  3. MAKE THE BROTH: In a medium pot, combine the clam juice and chicken broth. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat and remove the pot from the heat. Add the soy sauce and salt and stir to combine. Taste and season with more salt, if desired. Cover and keep warm over low heat.
  4. SOFT BOILED EGGS: Fill a large bowl with water and ice. Bring a medium pot of water to a boil, then reduce to simmer. Using a large spoon, gently add the eggs one at a time. Cook for 7 minutes. Transfer the eggs to the bowl with the ice water. Peel when ready to serve.
  5. TO SERVER: Divide the scallion-garlic oil among four deep bowls. Add 1 teaspoon sesame oil to each bowl.
  6. When the water is boiling, add the ramen noodles and cook according to the package directions. Drain the noodles and divide among the four bowls.
  7. To each bowl, add about 2 cups of the broth. Halve the soft-boiled eggs. Top each bowl with the braised pork belly, eggs, chopped scallion, and a slice of butter.

Tips:

  1. Try to find clam juice in a glass bottle. Clam juice in a can might have a slight metallic flavor.
  2. The saltiness of clam juice and chicken broth varies depending on the brand, so make sure to taste the broth and adjust the amount of salt to your liking.
