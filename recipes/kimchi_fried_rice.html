Kimchi Fried Rice

Description

Serving Size: 2

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 pound of slab bacon, roughly chopped
  • 1 medium onion, cut into small dice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch knob ginger, minced
  • 1.5 cups chopped extra-aged napa cabbage kimchi
  • 2 cups cooked rice, preferably day-old
  • 1 tablespoon gochugang
  • 2 tablespoon butter, softened
  • 2 eggs, fried sunny-side up
  • 2 scallions, sliced thinly

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, wok or cast-iron pan over high heat, cook the bacon, stirring, until fat is fully rendered and the bacon is barely starting to crisp. Pour out all but 2 tablespoons fat.
  2. Add onion, garlic and ginger and saute for 1 minute, or until very aromatic. Add chopped kimchi and rice and saute,, stirring frequently, for 4 to 50 minutes, or until very hot. Drop the heat to medium-low and flatten the rice with your spatula. COntinue to cook until the bottom layer is crispy, about 2 minutes; think Spanish paella here. The longer you leave it, the more crispy the bottom will become, but be careful not to burn the garlic.
  3. While the rice continues to crisp, in a small bowl mix together the gochugang and softened butter.
  4. Server from the pan or wok, topped with fried eggs, sliced scallions and gochugang butter.
