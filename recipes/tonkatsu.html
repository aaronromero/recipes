Pork cutlets

Serving Size: 4

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 40 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups panko bread crumbs
  • 1 pound prok tenderloin
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Neutral oil, such as canola or grapeseed, for frying
  • Flaky sea salt, such as Maldon, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the eggs and 1/4 cup water. Using copsticks or a fork, whisk until blended. Add teh flour and mix well to combine. Set aside. Place the panko in a shallow dish and set aside.
  2. Slice the pork crosswise into 1-inch-thick medallions. You should have about 8 medallions. Using the back of yyour kniife, gently pound both sides of the pork to flatten it until it is 1/2 inch thick. Season both sides with kosher salt and pepper.
  3. Working with one piece at a time, dip the pork into the egg-flour mixture, allowing any excess to drain off. Press the porn into the panko to coat both sides. Trasnfer to a plate.
  4. Line a large palte with paper towels. Pour about 1 inch of oil into a large deep port or Dutch oven. Heat the oil over medium heat until it registers 340F. If you dont have a thermometer, add a few crumbs of panko to the oil. The oil the right temperature when the crumbs immediately rise to the surface.
  5. Working in batches, fry the pork, flipping once or twice, until cooked through and golden brown, 3 to 4 minutes. Transfer the pork cutlets to the paper towels to drain.
  6. Sprinkle the pork cutlets with flaky sea salt. The port cutlets will keep in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
