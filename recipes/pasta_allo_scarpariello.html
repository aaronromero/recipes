Pasta Allo Scarpariello

Pasta allo scarpariello is the classic Neapolitan pasta with tomato sauce, which can be exceptional if done right. The dish is named after the humble shoemakers who inhabited the impoverished Spagnoli quarter of Naples, as they used to take the humble ingredients of this meal for payment. If you dont want to use spaghetti, ziti spezzati and penne are good options.

Serving Size: 4

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 20 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1/2 mild chile pepper, stem and seeds removed, minced
  • 1 lb cherry tomatoes (datterini recommended), halved
  • 14 oz spaghetti
  • Large handful of fresh basil
  • 6 tblspn grated pecorino romano cheese
  • 6 tblspn grated parmigiano reggiano cheese

Instructions:

  1. Bring water to a boil in a large saucepan.
  2. Place the olive oil in a large skillet and warm it over medium-low heat. Add teh garlic and cook for 2 minutes. Add the chile and cook for 1 minutes.
  3. Add teh tomatoes, raise the heat to medium, and cook, stirring occassionally, until the tomatoes start to collapse, about 10 minutes.
  4. Add salt to the boiling water, let the water return to a full boil, and add pasta. Cook until the past is very al dente. Reserve 2 cups pasta water and drain the spaghetti.
  5. Add the basil and some of the pasta water to the skillet, season the sauce with salt, and remove the garlic clove.
  6. Add the pasta and more pasta water (if desired) and toss until combined and the pasta is al dente.
  7. Turn off the heat, add the cheeses, and toss to combine. Server immediately.
